# Good Run

It's been a few months. May 2025 to April 2026. About a year.

I left a dev role at a fintech to join. I'd already been contributing to the Flutter SDK on the side, so when the opportunity came up it made sense. I was part of the Frontline Triage team, triaging issues in the flutter/flutter repo for Google. Day to day it was reading crash logs, reproducing bugs, filing reports, occasionally a PR. You'd get an issue, figure out if it was real, gather enough info for the engineering team to act on it, then move to the next one. Not the most exciting work, but you get fast at it. The team was small and mostly async across time zones. It worked better than it probably should have.

After nine months, the triage work wound down. I moved to dev support. Helping developers debug build and deployment issues on their CI/CD platform. Different kind of problem-solving: less open source repo, more "why is my pipeline failing at 2am." The shift from triage to dev support meant different expectations on both sides, which made it harder to find footing. Work wasn't the only thing that made those months hard. I gave it what I could, but three months later it ended. They were generous about it, and I appreciate that.

My teammate Daniel has a full write-up on his time there, [worth a read](https://danagbemava.github.io/posts/2026-05-22-reflections/). He asked whether the work actually mattered if no one remembers you after you're gone. I keep coming back to that. I think it did. The triage standards in flutter/flutter didn't write themselves.

Taking some time off now. Revamped the blog, caught up on AI. Open to opportunities. Flutter, mobile, whatever makes sense. Drop me a message if something comes to mind.
